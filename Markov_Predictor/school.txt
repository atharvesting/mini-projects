Every day at school begins the same way for Tim. He wakes up early when the alarm clock rings loud in his room. Tim gets out of bed and rubs his eyes. He brushes his teeth in the bathroom and washes his face with cold water. Then Tim puts on his school uniform, the blue shirt and gray pants. Downstairs in the kitchen, Mom has breakfast ready on the table. Tim eats toast with butter and drinks a glass of milk. He grabs his school bag and checks for books, pens, and homework inside.

The school bus comes at seven thirty sharp every morning. Tim runs out the door and waves to Mom before climbing on board. The bus ride takes twenty minutes through busy streets. Tim sits by the window and watches cars, shops, and people walking fast. At school, everyone lines up in the playground before the bell rings. Tim stands with his friends and talks about homework or games. The teacher takes attendance and leads the class to their room.

First period is math class every day of the week. Tim opens his notebook and listens to the teacher explain numbers and shapes. He writes problems on the page and tries to solve them quick. Sometimes Tim raises his hand to ask questions when he doesn't understand. The teacher helps and shows examples on the board. After math comes English class where they read stories and write sentences. Tim likes stories about adventures and animals best. He copies new words in his vocabulary book and practices spelling them right.

At recess, Tim runs to the field with his friends to play football. They kick the ball back and forth across the grass. Tim scores a goal and everyone cheers loud. The whistle blows after fifteen minutes and they line up again. Science class is next with experiments and diagrams. Tim mixes colors in test tubes and watches them change. The teacher talks about plants, water, and stars in the sky. Tim draws pictures of leaves and flowers in his science notebook.

Lunch time comes at noon every school day. Tim sits at a table in the cafeteria with his lunch box. He eats rice, vegetables, and chicken that Mom packed neat. His friends share jokes and trade snacks while they eat. After lunch, they wash hands and go to the playground again. Tim swings on the bars or slides down the big slide. The bell rings and everyone hurries back to class.

Afternoon classes include history and art every week. In history, the teacher tells stories about kings, wars, and old cities. Tim listens and takes notes about dates and names. Art class is fun where they paint pictures and make crafts. Tim draws a house with trees and a sun shining bright. He uses red, blue, and yellow colors on the paper. The teacher hangs the best drawings on the wall for everyone to see.

School ends at three o'clock sharp each day. Tim waits for the bus in line with his bag on his back. The ride home feels shorter because he talks with friends about the day. At home, Tim changes out of uniform and does homework first. He sits at his desk with books open and pencils sharp. Mom checks his work and helps with hard questions. Dinner is at six with the family around the table. Tim eats dal, roti, and salad while Dad asks about school.

After dinner, Tim plays video games or reads comics for one hour. He finishes with a bath and puts on pajamas soft. Bedtime comes at nine when lights go out in the house. Tim thinks about school tomorrow and closes his eyes. The alarm will ring again and the day starts the same. Tim wakes up, eats breakfast, rides the bus, learns math and English, plays at recess, eats lunch, paints in art, and comes home tired. Every school day follows this routine just like clockwork. Tim likes the rhythm and looks forward to friends and classes each time. Week after week, the schedule stays steady and fun.
